Freelance pricing, without the panic

A softer way to handle the business side of creative work.

This toolkit is made for artists, illustrators, designers, and creative freelancers who want practical pricing guidance without feeling like they need an MBA. Start with a range, set a boundary, ask for a deposit, and send the invoice.

How much should I charge as a creative freelancer?

Start with the project type, your experience, the client’s usage, and how many rounds of revisions are included. Project pricing is often easier than hourly pricing for creative work.

Should artists charge a deposit?

Yes. A 30–50% deposit is common for custom creative work and helps protect your time before you begin concepts, sketches, or design exploration.

What if a client says my price is too high?

You can offer to reduce the scope, but avoid lowering the price without changing the deliverables. A smaller package is usually better than a discounted full project.

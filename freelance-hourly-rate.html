Average Freelance Hourly Rates

Here’s a rough breakdown of common freelance hourly rates:

• Beginner: $20 – $40/hour

• Intermediate: $40 – $80/hour

• Experienced: $80 – $150/hour

• Specialist: $150+/hour

These ranges vary depending on industry, location, and specialization.

Why Hourly Rates Are Often Misleading

Most freelancers don’t bill every hour they work. A significant portion of time goes to:

• emails and communication

• revisions and feedback

• proposals and client acquisition

• admin and organization

Because of this, your effective hourly rate needs to be higher than you might expect.

How to Calculate Your Hourly Rate

Instead of copying average rates, start with your personal numbers:

• desired income

• monthly expenses

• realistic working hours

• billable percentage

Then divide your total income needs by your actual billable hours.

Hourly vs Project Pricing

Many freelancers eventually move away from hourly pricing and toward project pricing. Project pricing:

• is easier for clients to understand

• protects your time

• allows you to earn more for efficiency

A strong approach is to use your hourly rate as a baseline, then convert it into project-based pricing.

When to Raise Your Rates

You should consider raising your rates when:

• you are consistently booked

• your projects take less time due to experience

• clients are accepting your prices without hesitation

Raising your rates is often necessary to maintain sustainable income.

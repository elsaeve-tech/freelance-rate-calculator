Freelance Pricing FAQ

How much should I charge as a freelancer?

Your freelance rate should be based on your income goals, expenses, and how many hours you can realistically bill. Many freelancers undercharge because they don’t account for non-billable time like emails, revisions, and admin work.

What is a good freelance hourly rate?

A typical freelance hourly rate can range from $20 to $150+ depending on experience and industry. However, the most accurate rate is one based on your personal financial needs and workload.

Should I charge hourly or per project?

Many freelancers prefer project pricing because it protects their time and is easier for clients to understand. Hourly pricing can still work for smaller or ongoing tasks.

Why do freelancers undercharge?

Freelancers often underestimate how much of their time is unpaid. When non-billable work is ignored, rates end up being too low to sustain a stable income.

How do I know if I am undercharging?

If you feel busy but your income isn’t matching your effort, your rate is likely too low. This usually means your billable hours are lower than expected or your pricing doesn’t reflect your true workload.

Freelance Pricing Examples

How much should I charge for a logo?

Logo pricing can range from $100 to $5,000+ depending on experience, revisions, and usage rights. Many freelancers undercharge because they don’t factor in concept development and revisions.

How much should I charge for a website?

Website projects can range from $1,000 to $10,000+ depending on complexity, pages, and functionality. Pricing should reflect not just build time, but communication and revisions.

How much should I charge per hour as a beginner?

Beginner freelance rates typically range from $20 to $40 per hour, but should still be based on your expenses and billable time to avoid burnout.

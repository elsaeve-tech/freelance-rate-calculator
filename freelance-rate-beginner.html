Freelance Rate for Beginners (2026)

What to charge when you're just starting out — without underpricing yourself.

One of the most common questions new freelancers ask is: “How much should I charge?”

Many beginners default to very low prices to get clients, but this often leads to burnout and unsustainable income.

Typical Beginner Freelance Rates

Beginner freelance rates vary depending on skill and industry, but a general range is:

• $20 – $40 per hour

However, this is only a rough guideline. What matters more is whether your rate actually supports your time and expenses.

Why Beginners Often Undercharge

New freelancers tend to:

• feel unsure about their value

• price based on fear of losing clients

• underestimate how long work takes

• ignore non-billable time

These factors lead to rates that are too low to sustain long-term freelance work.

A Better Way to Set Your Rate

Even as a beginner, your pricing should still be based on:

• your income needs

• your expenses

• your available working hours

• how much of your time is billable

Instead of guessing, calculate a baseline rate and adjust from there.

Use the freelance rate calculator to get a realistic starting point.

Should Beginners Charge Hourly or Per Project?

Hourly pricing can be easier when starting out, especially if you're unsure how long projects will take.

However, project pricing often becomes more effective over time because it allows you to earn more as you improve.

How to Increase Your Rates Over Time

As you gain experience, you can raise your rates by:

• improving your portfolio

• working with better clients

• specializing in a niche

• delivering results more efficiently

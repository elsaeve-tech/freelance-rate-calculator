How much to charge for real freelance projects — without guessing.

One of the hardest parts of freelancing is figuring out what to charge for a project. Most freelancers either underprice to secure the client or overthink it and hesitate to send the quote.

How to Price a Freelance Project

A strong project price usually starts with a daily or hourly rate, then adjusts for:

• project complexity

• number of revisions

• timeline / urgency

• client type (individual vs business)

• usage or licensing (for creative work)

Freelance Project Pricing Examples

Logo design

• Beginner: $100 – $500

• Intermediate: $500 – $1,500

• Experienced: $1,500 – $5,000+

Website design

• Basic: $1,000 – $3,000

• Standard: $3,000 – $8,000

• Advanced: $8,000 – $20,000+

Illustration / creative work

• Small project: $100 – $500

• Mid-size: $500 – $2,000

• Large / commercial: $2,000+

Why Many Freelancers Underprice Projects

Project pricing often feels harder than hourly pricing because it involves uncertainty. Many freelancers:

• underestimate how long work takes

• don’t account for revisions

• feel pressure to “win” the client

This leads to underpricing and burnout over time.

A Better Way to Price Projects

Instead of guessing, calculate your hourly or daily rate first. Then multiply it by the estimated time required for the project.

From there, adjust based on value, complexity, and risk.

Quick Pricing Formula

A simple starting formula:

Project Price = Daily Rate × Number of Days + Buffer

Always include a buffer for revisions, delays, and communication.
